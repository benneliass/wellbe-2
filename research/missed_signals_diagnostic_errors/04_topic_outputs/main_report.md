# Main report: Research B - missed signals, diagnostic errors, and late discovery cases

## Summary

The completed research collected documented cases and patterns where health problems were missed, dismissed, delayed, or discovered late. The evidence points to repeated black zones: intake, triage, first assessment, lab/radiology follow-up, discharge, referral, repeat visits, and specialist review.

## Method

This package reorganizes the completed workbook into CSV evidence tables and Markdown summaries. No new research was added.

## Source overview

The source log contains 15 sources across medical literature, patient-safety cases, public reports, advocacy/patient stories, and low-resource health-system documentation.

## Key findings

- **symptom dismissed** appeared in 6 case rows. Patient's stated severity or persistence is treated as low-signal.
- **mental-health misattribution** appeared in 3 case rows. Physical illness is explained as anxiety/panic/stress before high-risk alternatives are ruled out.
- **normal-test trap** appeared in 7 case rows. A normal prior/current test is overgeneralized beyond its scope or timing.
- **abnormal result not followed up** appeared in 9 case rows. Abnormal lab/imaging/result exists but does not become action.
- **repeated visits ignored** appeared in 7 case rows. Return visits are handled as new isolated encounters instead of escalation signals.
- **no one connected the dots** appeared in 7 case rows. Signals distributed across time, specialties, or notes never assemble into a pattern.
- **poor handoff** appeared in 6 case rows. Important data crosses team/shift/setting boundaries without reliable receipt/action.
- **no follow-up ownership** appeared in 7 case rows. A necessary next step exists, but no named accountable person owns it.
- **referral delay** appeared in 7 case rows. Patient needs another expertise/pathway but referral is late, absent, or not completed.
- **red flag missed** appeared in 9 case rows. Time-sensitive warning sign is present but not elevated.
- **patient self-advocacy required** appeared in 6 case rows. Diagnosis advances only after patient/family insists, researches, or returns repeatedly.
- **low-resource diagnostic supply gap** appeared in 3 case rows. The test/referral needed to identify disease is unavailable or unreliable.
