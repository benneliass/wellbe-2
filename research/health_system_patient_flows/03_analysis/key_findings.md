# Key findings

1. Front-door workflows usually begin as risk sorting, not full diagnosis.
2. Diagnostic loss often occurs after the first encounter: handoffs, pending results, referrals, discharge, and follow-up ownership.
3. Repeat visits are a major warning signal, but many systems treat visits as separate episodes.
4. Normal-test traps appear across primary care, ED, specialist, and outpatient diagnostics.
5. Low-resource systems often identify illness through community health workers, home visits, syndromic algorithms, or screening before formal clinician assessment.
6. Patient self-advocacy is repeatedly documented in delayed or missed complex conditions.
