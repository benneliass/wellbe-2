# Researched Facts

Facts are separated from interpretation. Each fact maps to source IDs in `01_source_log/source_log.csv`.

## F001: Terminology

**Source IDs:** S001

**Fact:** In clinical/biological contexts, second brain can refer to the enteric nervous system in the gut.

**Product relevance:** This means product naming should clarify that the platform is a medical-memory concept, not gut-brain biology.

## F002: Generic second brain

**Source IDs:** S002

**Fact:** Popular second-brain systems focus on capturing, organizing, distilling, and using knowledge later.

**Product relevance:** Healthcare needs a modified model because medical memory must handle safety, uncertainty, responsibility, and review.

## F003: Diagnostic process

**Source IDs:** S003

**Fact:** Diagnosis is collaborative and system-dependent.

**Product relevance:** A health platform should support patient-clinician-team memory, not just individual patient storage.

## F004: Patient digital records

**Source IDs:** S004

**Fact:** Patient-centered digital health records show possible benefits but evidence remains mixed/needs stronger studies.

**Product relevance:** Do not assume record access alone solves diagnostic continuity.

## F005: Patient correction

**Source IDs:** S005;S020

**Fact:** Shared notes can help patients identify errors, gaps, and safety blind spots.

**Product relevance:** Patient correction should be built into the product as a safety loop.

## F006: Loop closure

**Source IDs:** S006

**Fact:** Portal access and reading notes can be associated with better test/referral loop closure.

**Product relevance:** Patient-visible follow-up status may improve safety, but gaps remain.

## F007: Patient co-authoring

**Source IDs:** S007

**Fact:** Patients can submit previsit agenda/history information through notes/portals.

**Product relevance:** Previsit Health Thread updates are feasible.

## F008: Patient diagnostic input

**Source IDs:** S008

**Fact:** Patient/family contributions can reveal diagnostic-process risks such as repeat visits, access barriers, test/referral issues, coordination issues, and communication breakdowns.

**Product relevance:** Patient story should be treated as diagnostic-safety evidence, not just subjective narrative.

## F009: Problem lists

**Source IDs:** S009

**Fact:** Problem lists and longitudinal records are important but vary in quality and need curation.

**Product relevance:** Health Threads should not simply duplicate a problem list; they should curate unresolved issues.

## F010: Test results

**Source IDs:** S010

**Fact:** Closed-loop test-result communication depends on technology plus responsibility and process clarity.

**Product relevance:** A memory platform must assign owners and states, not just display results.

## F011: Follow-up failures

**Source IDs:** S011;S012

**Fact:** Missed imaging and lab follow-up can contribute to diagnostic delays or harm.

**Product relevance:** Pending/abnormal-result tracking should be a core safety feature.

## F012: PGHD workflow

**Source IDs:** S013;S014

**Fact:** Patient-generated health data is useful only when integrated into clinician workflow with clear responsibilities and trend-based views.

**Product relevance:** The platform should summarize trends and baseline changes, not dump raw logs.

## F013: Health IT risk

**Source IDs:** S015

**Fact:** Electronic record systems can themselves create missed, delayed, or incorrect care.

**Product relevance:** Implementation must include usability testing, feedback, safety monitoring, and governance.

## F014: Clinical safety

**Source IDs:** S016

**Fact:** Digital health tools require clinical risk management and documented safety assurance.

**Product relevance:** High-risk features need hazard analysis and named clinical safety ownership.

## F015: Regulation

**Source IDs:** S017

**Fact:** Clinical decision support may fall under regulatory oversight depending on function, transparency, and intended use.

**Product relevance:** Avoid early claims of autonomous diagnosis; prioritize memory, communication, and follow-up support.

## F016: Interoperability

**Source IDs:** S018;S019

**Fact:** FHIR/SMART APIs can support access to patient records such as problems, observations, labs, notes, and documents.

**Product relevance:** Technical implementation is possible, but integration and permissions vary by market.

## F017: Low-resource implementation

**Source IDs:** S021

**Fact:** Digital health initiatives require organizational, human, technological, and financial capacity.

**Product relevance:** Low-resource adaptations may need printable summaries, SMS, CHW support, and offline-friendly workflows.
