# Main report: Research D — doctor, nurse, and staff views

## Summary

Clinician-side diagnostic pain points are concentrated around time pressure, EHR burden, fragmented records, unclear ownership after tests/referrals, triage overload, handoff failures, fatigue, bias, and limited diagnostic access. The strongest sources frame diagnosis as a collaborative system process rather than a single event.

## Source overview

The archive contains 24 tracked sources: official safety reports, federal/global frameworks, peer-reviewed studies, systematic reviews, qualitative studies, and one clinician commentary source.

## Key findings

- EHR inboxes and alerts can hide abnormal results.
- Repeated visits and unresolved symptoms are not always surfaced.
- Diagnostic uncertainty needs clearer follow-up ownership.
- Triage, handover, and referral are diagnostic-safety stages.
- Staff fatigue and cognitive overload are safety risks.
- Digital intake can lose complexity if it forces single-symptom routing.
- Low-resource settings need resource-aware diagnostic workflows.

## Important examples

- HSSIB's Brian case: eight-month delay before spinal metastatic cancer diagnosis after repeated contacts.
- HSSIB online consultation case: prior skin-cancer history and worsening symptoms were not joined during online triage.
- Ghana handover study: poor communication and documentation during nurse handovers created patient-safety risk.
