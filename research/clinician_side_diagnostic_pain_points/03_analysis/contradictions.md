# Contradictions and different realities

- ED diagnostic-error literature highlights high-risk missed conditions, while ED clinician commentary emphasizes crowding and systemic overload as the broader safety problem.
- Structured online intake can capture red flags, but can also force patients into single-symptom routes that lose complexity.
- Free-text patient narratives preserve nuance, but may omit clinical decision-critical details.
- EHRs can support longitudinal tracking, but current EHR designs can also create alert overload, note bloat, and cognitive burden.
