### Main limitations carried forward

- Raw webpages/PDFs/screenshots were generally not downloaded in the packages; source-summary files and URLs were preserved instead.
- Patient complaints and advocacy stories are valuable workflow signals but are not always clinically adjudicated.
- Non-English and some regional evidence remains underrepresented.
- The synthesis is not a prevalence estimate, systematic review, or validated clinical decision-support model.
- Some source dates/authors are missing or approximate in the packages.


## Executive synthesis

The product should not be a one-shot symptom checker. The evidence supports a longitudinal, workflow-embedded diagnostic-safety layer that helps patients explain what changed, helps clinicians see the relevant episode quickly, and closes loops around tests, referrals, discharge and follow-up. The highest-confidence opportunities are: diagnostic episode tracking, repeat-visit reset, abnormal-result/referral ownership, normal-test safety netting, patient voice protection, and a concise clinician summary. Region-specific modules are essential for CHW/community pathways, low-resource diagnostics, language/cultural safety and fragmented public/private systems.

## 10. Implementation blueprint

### MVP sequence

1. **MVP 1: Patient intake + clinician summary** — timeline, change-from-baseline, patient quote, prior tests/diagnoses/family history, doctor-facing summary with missing data checklist.
2. **MVP 2: Diagnostic episode and repeat-visit reset** — cluster unresolved symptoms across visits, surface repeat visits, capture what remains unexplained and safety-net plan.
3. **MVP 3: Closed-loop tasks** — abnormal-result ledger, pending-test discharge contract, referral completion tracker.
4. **MVP 4: Region modules** — language/cultural safety, patient-held/out-of-system records, CHW/informal referral capture, low-resource stockout-aware workflows.
5. **MVP 5: Advanced safety layer** — lab-trend explorer, note-diff, bias/misattribution guardrail, workload-aware worklists; deploy only with safety monitoring and local validation.

### Measures to track

- Percentage of abnormal results with owner, patient notification and closure.
- Percentage of referrals with status beyond “sent”: accepted/rejected/advice-only/scheduled/attended/report returned.
- Repeat-visit diagnostic reset completion rate and override reasons.
- Patient-reported “my main concern was captured” and “I know what happens next.”
- Alert volume, false positives, false negatives and clinician time burden.
- Disparities by language, digital access, geography and cultural-safety indicators, using privacy-preserving methods.
