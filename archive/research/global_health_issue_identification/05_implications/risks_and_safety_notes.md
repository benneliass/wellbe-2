# Risks and safety notes

- A product that over-trusts a single normal test can reinforce diagnostic delay.
- A product that assumes a stable primary-care owner will fail in open-access and fragmented systems.
- Automated triage can reproduce bias if it ignores racism, cultural safety, language barriers, and patient/family concern.
- Patient-held records can improve continuity but need privacy, consent, and data-quality safeguards.
- CHW tools must not assign responsibility without training, supplies, supervision, and referral backup.
- Design must avoid blaming patients for missed follow-up when cost, transport, entitlement, or system delays are the real barrier.
