# Contradictions and different realities

- Some missed-signal cases involved abundant data that no one connected; others involved low-resource contexts where the diagnostic input was never available.
- Normal results were sometimes genuine reassurance and sometimes misleading because the test was old, wrong-scope, or performed outside a symptomatic window.
- Patient advocacy stories strongly surface dismissal and invalidation, while institutional safety cases more often frame failures as systems, handoffs, or result-management problems.
