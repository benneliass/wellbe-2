- **symptom dismissed**: Patient's stated severity or persistence is treated as low-signal. Product implication: Capture patient narrative verbatim, severity, duration, and change-from-baseline; surface when repeated.
- **mental-health misattribution**: Physical illness is explained as anxiety/panic/stress before high-risk alternatives are ruled out. Product implication: Add psychiatric-attribution guardrail requiring physical red-flag review and objective next step.
- **normal-test trap**: A normal prior/current test is overgeneralized beyond its scope or timing. Product implication: Show test limitations: date, body region, symptom status, sensitivity, and what it does not rule out.
- **abnormal result not followed up**: Abnormal lab/imaging/result exists but does not become action. Product implication: Closed-loop result ownership with acknowledgment, action, patient notification, and closure.
- **repeated visits ignored**: Return visits are handled as new isolated encounters instead of escalation signals. Product implication: Repeat-visit detector that displays recent encounters and forces diagnostic reset.
- **no one connected the dots**: Signals distributed across time, specialties, or notes never assemble into a pattern. Product implication: Longitudinal problem map that clusters symptoms, labs, visits, family history, and specialty notes.
- **poor handoff**: Important data crosses team/shift/setting boundaries without reliable receipt/action. Product implication: Handoff payloads with required critical fields and receiving-team acknowledgment.
- **no follow-up ownership**: A necessary next step exists, but no named accountable person owns it. Product implication: Assign owner/deadline/escalation for every pending test, referral, and critical result.
- **referral delay**: Patient needs another expertise/pathway but referral is late, absent, or not completed. Product implication: Referral tracker that verifies booking, attendance, result, and next-step communication.
- **red flag missed**: Time-sensitive warning sign is present but not elevated. Product implication: Red-flag rules tied to stage-specific workflows: triage, imaging, labs, discharge.
- **patient self-advocacy required**: Diagnosis advances only after patient/family insists, researches, or returns repeatedly. Product implication: Let patients submit structured concerns, photos, family history, and 'what I fear is being missed.'
- **low-resource diagnostic supply gap**: The test/referral needed to identify disease is unavailable or unreliable. Product implication: Stockout-aware clinical pathways, referral alternatives, and supply-chain alerting.
