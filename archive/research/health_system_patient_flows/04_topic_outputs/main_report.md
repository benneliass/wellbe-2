# Main report: Real health-system patient flows for identifying health issues

## Summary
This research package documents real-world workflows for identifying health issues across health-system entry points and care settings. It focuses on what actually happens at intake, triage, clinician assessment, testing, referral, discharge, repeat visit, specialist review, screening, and community follow-up.

## Source overview
The archive contains 56 sources and 53 extracted evidence items. Sources include guidelines, clinical workflow tools, public health reports, patient-safety investigations, diagnostic-error studies, advocacy reports, and patient-experience materials.

## Key findings
- Most front-door workflows sort urgency before they synthesize diagnosis.
- Important signals often disappear in black zones: normal-test trap, abnormal result not followed up, referral delay, poor handoff, fragmented care, repeated visits ignored, and no follow-up ownership.
- Low-resource/community workflows often start before the clinic visit through home visits, CHWs, empanelment, or screening.
- Chronic, hidden, rare, or fluctuating conditions are especially vulnerable to minimization and disconnected episodes.
