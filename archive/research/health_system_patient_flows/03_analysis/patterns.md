# Patterns across sources

- Entry point determines what data is visible: emergency triage prioritizes danger, primary care prioritizes reason for encounter, screening prioritizes eligibility/risk, and CHW systems prioritize household/community risk.
- Vitals and red flags are strong for acute deterioration but weak for hidden, chronic, rare, or fluctuating conditions.
- Referral is not a single step; it includes creation, triage, acceptance/rejection, scheduling, patient attendance, and result/report return.
- A result can be technically available but functionally invisible if no person owns follow-up.
- Patient narratives often reveal continuity failures earlier than structured records.
